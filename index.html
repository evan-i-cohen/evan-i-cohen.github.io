Story of My Previous Research Experiences

I spent the summer of my freshman year working in a physical chemistry lab. I wasn't that good at labwork so I ended up writing a Java plugin for ImageJ to track and analyze the intensity of fluorescent quantum dots over time. After deciding wet labwork wasn't for me, I spent the summer of my sophomore year in a computational chemistry lab. The programming is done in C++ for performance. I added functionality to a dipole moment calculator but spent most of my time generating and analyzing I/O files with the aid of python and bash. I improved my UNIX skills and now feel comfortable with tools like ssh, bash, grep, and screen, vim , and aliasing. I realized coding was my favorite part of these experiences and decided to double major in CS and apply to internships. Not too difficult, since I was already getting a CS minor and a major is only 3 extra classes.
